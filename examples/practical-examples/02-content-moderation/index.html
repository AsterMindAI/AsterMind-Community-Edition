🛡️ Content Moderation

Real-time toxicity detection powered by AsterMind ELM. Privacy-first, on-device moderation.

What This Demo Does

This demo classifies text as Safe, Warning, or Unsafe based on toxicity and appropriateness. It learns from your feedback - give it a thumbs up/down and it retrains automatically, getting smarter over time. The model understands context, not just keywords, so it can distinguish between "I hate this product" (warning) and "I hate you" (unsafe).

Why AsterMind Makes This Possible

Hard-coded logic can't do this: You'd need to maintain massive blacklists of profanity, create complex regex patterns for every variation, and still miss context. "This is fucking amazing" vs "fuck you" - same word, different intent. Hard-coded rules can't understand sarcasm, context, or cultural nuances. You'd need thousands of if-statements and still fail on edge cases.

AsterMind enables: The ELM classifier learns patterns from examples, understanding semantic relationships and context. It can distinguish between negative feedback and personal attacks, recognize profanity in context, and adapt to your specific moderation needs through feedback. The model learns that "stupid product" (warning) is different from "you're stupid" (unsafe) - something impossible with keyword matching. Plus, it improves with each feedback, learning your specific standards automatically.
